Differences Between Licenses, Permits, and Inspections

Licenses:

Licenses are the city’s way of keeping track of businesses, establishing a legal connection between businesses and their owners, and regulating certain types of commerce. The license fee is a tax that helps cover the cost of these and other services that the city provides to businesses.

Permits:

Permits are proof that your building and facilities follow city rules ("code") and are safe for you, your employees, and your customers.

Inspections:

As you complete each phase of construction, you'll need to have city inspectors visit to ensure that the work meets the health and safety standards in the Long Beach city code. Some inspections are performed before approval of a business license to ensure the site meets the health and safety standards of the City.

Applying for a Business License

You can obtain a business license by going to the 4th floor of Long Beach City Hall. Fill out the first page of the business license application and bring it to the Business License counter.

Annual vs. Term Licenses

Annual Licenses

A business license is valid for one year from the date it was issued. It must be renewed each year. A renewal notice is sent to the license holder 14 days prior to the due date, and they have 30 days to pay without penalty. You are still responsible for payment by the due date even if you do not receive a notice. If you change your mailing address during the year, contact the Business License Department to report the change.

Short Term License

If your business location is in another city and you plan to do work in Long Beach, you are required to purchase an ‘out of city’ business license. The City recognizes the work may not last an entire year and offers pro-rated term licenses in three month increments.

Renewal and Late Fees

You have 14 days prior to the due date to pay the renewal and then 30 days after the 14 days before a penalty is assessed. It is not renewed automatically. At the end of the license term you paid for, the license automatically closes. You need to contact us via email, phone or in person to request the account be reactivated and pay the tax assessment to get the new license.

Additional Special Licenses

Some business types require additional special licenses to operate. Examples include food preparation, alcohol sales, tobacco sales, or massage services. City staff can tell you what licenses your business might need in order to operate.

Within those classifications, Long Beach has around 250 different business license categories. The classifications drive our overall tax assessment structure. For each different classification a license would be required. For example, if you had a restaurant and had 2 pool tables in that restaurant that would fall under 2 classifications: one would be the restaurant, which would fall under class “I” (Retailing) and the pool tables would fall under class “M” (Unique Business), so in this scenario there would be 2 licenses required.

Business Improvement District Fees

Businesses located in Business Improvement Districts (BIDs) must pay additional fees in order to pay for the extra services that the BIDs provide to businesses in these areas.

These fees are collected by the City and passed on to the BIDs for uses like street cleaning, special events, or local beautification. Learn more about BIDs here: Step 3: Find a Location .

Once I file an application, is the information available to the public?

All information on the Business License application is public record except information like SSN, Fed tax ID's and CDL #'s would not be provided in a public records act request. A subpoena would be required to obtain that information.

Late Fee

You will be fined 25% of your amount due if you do not pay within 30 days of the due date. An additional 10% penalty will be added for each month the bill goes unpaid. The postmark date will determine whether or not your payment is delinquent.

Multiple Businesses at One Location

You are required to obtain an additional license for each business activity even if it is in the same location.

If you have more than one business license at the same location, you may choose to pay for all employees on one license. If so, you will pay for the employees on the license with the higher employee rate.

(L.B.M.C 3.80.420.6)

Business License Issued

In order to receive your business license, all reviews, documentation, and inspections need to be completed. Once your Business License application is processed, pay the fees at the Cashier’s counter, bring the receipt back to the Business License counter for confirmation, and you will be issued your Business License.
